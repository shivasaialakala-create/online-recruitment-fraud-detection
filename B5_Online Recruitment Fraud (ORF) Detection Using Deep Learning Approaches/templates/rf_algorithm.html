Details and Evaluation for Online Recruitment Fraud (ORF) Detection

Algorithm Overview

The **Random Forest (RF)** algorithm is a powerful **ensemble learning** technique used for both classification and regression tasks. It is fundamentally a collection, or "forest," of **Decision Trees**. By training each tree on a random subset of the data and features (a process called *bagging*), it significantly reduces the risk of **overfitting** that individual decision trees often face.

During prediction, the model utilizes the collective intelligence of the forest: for classification, it takes a **majority vote** from all trees; for regression, it averages their outputs. This combination of diversity and aggregation results in a highly robust, accurate, and generalized model.

Key Strengths
  • Robustness: Highly resistant to overfitting and works well with noisy data.
  • Feature Importance: Provides scores to rank the most influential features, which is crucial for interpretability.
  • Handles Non-Linearity: Excels at capturing complex, non-linear relationships in the data.
  • Parallel Processing: Trees are built independently, making the training process highly efficient and scalable.
  • Missing Values: Less sensitive to missing data compared to other algorithms.
